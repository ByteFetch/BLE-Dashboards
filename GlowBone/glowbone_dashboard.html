IMU Calibration

Calibration identifies the staff's long axis — which direction in IMU space runs from base to tip. This is required for Mode 10 (Bone Balance) and Mode 11 (Spin-Responsive Hue) to respond correctly to tilt and spin. With the IMU mounted at the bottom (readable left-to-right when the bone stands upright), the expected long axis after a successful calibration is Y+ (axis id 2).

Steps

  1. Press "Run IMU Cal" below. The firmware queues the routine — it starts at the next loop().
  2. Phase A — Hold still (≈3 s): Hold the staff vertical (tip up or tip down) and completely motionless. The LEDs pulse white during the 3-second hold — keep the bone still until you see a blue sweep. The firmware checks that one axis reads ≥0.7 g — it will red-flash and abort if you move during this phase.
  3. Phase B — Spin verify (≈2 s): When you see the cyan strobe begin, slowly spin the bone along its long axis (like twirling a baton). The firmware confirms that gyro rotation is dominant on the detected axis. If spin is too weak (<30°/s) it red-flashes and aborts.
  4. Result: Green flash = success — settings 11 (imu_long_axis) and 13 (imu_calibrated) written to NVS. Red flash = threshold not met — press Run again and retry.

After a successful calibration you can verify with Diagnostic D4: press Button 4 and cycle sub-modes until D4 (Direction Palette). Each of the 6 axis directions shows a distinct color. Point the tip toward the floor — it should show the color assigned to the detected axis. Use ◄► to manually step the axis override if needed.

Note: With the IMU mounted at the bottom (readable left-to-right when the bone stands up), the expected long axis after successful calibration is Y+ (axis id 2). The Direction Palette D4 sub-mode will show cyan/teal pointing up if calibration succeeded.
